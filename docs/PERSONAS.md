# Personas

## Purpose

These personas extend `PROJECT_SPEC.md` by describing visitor psychology. They should guide content, design, CTAs, CMS defaults, and homepage sequencing.

## Persona 1: Anna, 42, Experiencing Burnout

Anna has a responsible job, a family, and very little quiet. She is functioning, but she feels emotionally thin.

Goals:

- feel less overwhelmed
- understand why she is exhausted
- find a gentle first step

Concerns:

- "Will this be too spiritual for me?"
- "Will I be pressured to share?"
- "Do I have time?"

Website reassurance:

- calm homepage rhythm
- clear workshop expectations
- gentle language around burnout
- no exaggerated promises

## Persona 2: Marta And Piotr, Considering Relationship Work

They are not in crisis, but they repeat the same conversations and feel distance growing.

Goals:

- understand patterns
- communicate more safely
- find guided support

Concerns:

- "Will we be blamed?"
- "Is this therapy?"
- "Can we start without knowing exactly what we need?"

Website reassurance:

- language about patterns, listening, and relationship awareness
- FAQ explaining format and boundaries
- contact path for questions before booking

## Persona 3: Katarzyna, 38, Professional Leader Under Stress

Katarzyna leads others and is used to being competent. She is interested in inner work but dislikes vague wellness language.

Goals:

- regain clarity
- manage stress
- reconnect with values

Concerns:

- "Is this credible?"
- "Will it be practical?"
- "Will my time be respected?"

Website reassurance:

- structured offer pages
- practical workshop details
- professional, elegant design
- clear outcomes without guarantees

## Persona 4: Ewa, 31, Curious First-Time Visitor

Ewa found Joanna through social media or a friend. She is curious but unsure what W Centrum Duszy actually offers.

Goals:

- understand the brand quickly
- browse without commitment
- find content that feels relevant

Concerns:

- "Is this for me?"
- "Where should I start?"
- "Will I have to buy something?"

Website reassurance:

- homepage pathways
- beginner-friendly articles and meditations
- soft CTAs such as Read, Listen, Explore

## Persona 5: Returning Workshop Participant

This visitor has already met Joanna and wants to stay connected.

Goals:

- find upcoming workshops
- revisit meditations
- read new articles
- subscribe or book again

Concerns:

- "What's new?"
- "Can I find the next step easily?"

Website reassurance:

- clear upcoming events
- newsletter
- related content
- future client/course login

## Persona 6: Tomasz, 47, Completely Skeptical

Tomasz visits because someone recommended Joanna. He is wary of emotional language and anything that feels mystical.

Goals:

- check credibility
- understand exactly what happens
- decide whether this is serious

Concerns:

- "Is this vague?"
- "Is this too spiritual?"
- "Will I be manipulated?"

Website reassurance:

- plain language
- transparent offer structure
- grounded photography
- no mystical claims
- FAQ and contact details

## Persona 7: Natalia, 55, Life Transition

Natalia is navigating grief, children leaving home, divorce, relocation, or identity change. She wants depth but needs tenderness.

Goals:

- feel seen
- make meaning
- find a safe group or individual path

Concerns:

- "Will I become too emotional?"
- "Will I be held safely?"
- "Can I go slowly?"

Website reassurance:

- brand story
- clear emotional boundaries
- testimonials
- gentle descriptions of group work

## Persona 8: Corporate Or Community Organizer

This person may invite Joanna to lead a workshop for a team, group, or retreat.

Goals:

- understand Joanna's professionalism
- see themes and formats
- make contact

Concerns:

- "Can this work for our group?"
- "Is the tone appropriate?"
- "What does she need from us?"

Website reassurance:

- workshop formats
- professional language
- inquiry form with organizer option
- downloadable bio or future press kit
