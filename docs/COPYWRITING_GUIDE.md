# Copywriting Guide

## Purpose

This guide works with `VOICE_AND_TONE.md` to make every page feel like the same person and the same brand.

## Homepage

Write for arrival and orientation. The homepage should not explain everything at once.

Good headline direction:

"Return to the quiet center within you."

Good subtitle direction:

"W Centrum Duszy is Joanna Radek-Petersen's space for reflection, guided practice, workshops, and personal growth."

## About

The About page should build relational trust. Tell Joanna's story with humility, specificity, and warmth.

Avoid turning the biography into a list of credentials or a mystical origin story.

## Workshops

Workshop copy must answer:

- What is this about?
- Who is it for?
- What will happen?
- How should I prepare?
- What is the next step?

Use practical detail to create safety.

## Courses

Course copy should emphasize structure, pace, modules, support, and outcomes. Avoid promising transformation.

## Meditations

Meditation copy should be short and sensory. Include duration, theme, when to use it, and what the listener may notice.

## Articles

Articles should feel like thoughtful letters. Start with recognition, offer insight, then give one grounded practice or question.

## Contact

Contact copy should reduce hesitation.

Example:

"You are welcome to write with a question, a workshop inquiry, or a quiet first hello."

## CTA Buttons

Preferred:

- Contact Joanna
- Explore workshops
- Read the article
- Listen now
- Join the newsletter
- Ask a question
- Save my place

Avoid:

- Transform now
- Claim your spot
- Start healing
- Unlock access

## Navigation Labels

Use plain labels:

- Home
- About
- Workshops
- Courses
- Meditations
- Articles
- Gallery
- Contact

## Headlines

Headlines should be clear before poetic. If a headline is poetic, the subtitle must explain it.

## Subtitles

Subtitles should name the practical meaning of the page. Keep them under two sentences.

## FAQs

FAQ answers should be direct, reassuring, and practical. Do not hide important details in poetic language.

## Forms

Form labels should be human:

- Your name
- Email address
- What would you like to ask about?
- Message

Error message example:

"Please add your email address so Joanna can reply."

## 404 Page

Tone: calm and lightly human.

Suggested copy:

"This page is not here anymore. You can return to the homepage or explore Joanna's latest articles and workshops."

## Success Pages

Success pages should confirm what happened and what comes next.

Example:

"Thank you. Your message has been sent. Joanna will reply as soon as she can."

## Emails

Emails should be short, clear, and warm. Subject lines should be practical.

Examples:

- Your message to Joanna has been received
- Your workshop place request
- A quiet note from W Centrum Duszy
