# User Scroll Experience

## Purpose

This document turns the homepage architecture from `SITE_ARCHITECTURE.md` into an emotional journey. The homepage is not a list of sections. It is a sequence of psychological permissions.

## Emotional Arc

```text
arrival -> curiosity -> trust -> understanding -> connection -> hope -> action
```

## Section 1: Hero

Visitor thinks: "This feels calm. Who is Joanna, and is this for me?"

Visitor feels: slowed down, visually held, cautiously curious.

Why it exists: the hero establishes emotional safety before explanation. It should show Joanna or a strong brand image, the name W Centrum Duszy, and one clear promise.

Next action: choose between learning more or exploring offers.

## Section 2: Trust Introduction

Visitor thinks: "I understand the kind of space this is."

Visitor feels: reassured that the work is grounded and not exaggerated.

Why it exists: this section translates the brand promise into plain language. It should name inner calm, presence, patterns, relationships, and personal growth without overclaiming.

Next action: continue scrolling because the visitor feels oriented.

## Section 3: Offer Pathways

Visitor thinks: "There are different ways to begin."

Visitor feels: less pressure because there is not only one path.

Why it exists: visitors arrive with different readiness levels. Offer cards should make workshops, courses, meditations, and articles feel like equal doorways.

Next action: open the offer that matches their current need, or keep scrolling to understand Joanna first.

## Section 4: About Joanna Preview

Visitor thinks: "I want to know who is holding this space."

Visitor feels: more trust because a real person is visible.

Why it exists: personal growth work requires relational trust. This section should use Joanna's portrait and a short human introduction.

Next action: visit About or continue to see proof and practical offers.

## Section 5: Featured Workshop Or Course

Visitor thinks: "This is concrete. I can see what participation would look like."

Visitor feels: possibility with structure.

Why it exists: after trust, the site can present action. The featured offer should include date, format, theme, and who it is for.

Next action: view details, save a place, or ask a question.

## Section 6: Featured Meditation Or Article

Visitor thinks: "I can try something small before committing."

Visitor feels: relief because engagement can be low-risk.

Why it exists: many visitors need a quiet first contact. A meditation or article lets them experience Joanna's tone without booking.

Next action: read, listen, or subscribe.

## Section 7: Testimonials

Visitor thinks: "Other people have felt safe here."

Visitor feels: reassured, especially if skeptical or tender.

Why it exists: testimonials should reduce perceived risk. They must sound authentic and consent-based, not polished into marketing slogans.

Next action: continue toward contact or revisit an offer.

## Section 8: Newsletter Invitation

Visitor thinks: "I can stay close without deciding today."

Visitor feels: permission to move slowly.

Why it exists: the newsletter is a soft conversion for visitors who are not ready to book.

Next action: subscribe.

## Section 9: Contact Invitation

Visitor thinks: "I can ask a question. I know what to do next."

Visitor feels: clarity and gentle momentum.

Why it exists: the final section turns trust into an accessible next step. It should not feel like a closing sales pitch.

Next action: contact Joanna, view workshops, or return to an earlier pathway.

## Scroll Rhythm

The homepage should alternate between spacious emotional sections and practical clarity. Too much emotion becomes vague. Too much structure becomes cold.

Recommended rhythm:

```text
feeling -> explanation -> choice -> person -> offer -> sample -> proof -> relationship -> action
```
