# Visual Concept

## Role

This document translates the approved brand work into a complete visual experience. It extends `../BRAND_STORY.md`, `../BRAND_GUIDE.md`, `../DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`, `../USER_SCROLL_EXPERIENCE.md`, `../PHOTOGRAPHY_GUIDE.md`, and `../COPYWRITING_GUIDE.md`.

## First Impression

The visitor opens the homepage and the page feels quiet before it feels informative. The background is warm paper, not pure white. The logo mark appears with enough space to breathe. Navigation is present but not loud. A portrait of Joanna gives the visitor a real person to meet immediately.

The first viewport should feel like entering a calm room with natural light.

## Emotional Response

The visitor should feel:

- safe enough to keep reading
- curious without being pushed
- reassured that the brand is grounded
- aware that Joanna is human and present
- invited into a slower pace

Nothing should shout. Nothing should sparkle. Nothing should feel like a generic therapy template.

## Pacing

The visual rhythm follows the emotional arc defined in `../USER_SCROLL_EXPERIENCE.md`:

```text
arrival -> curiosity -> trust -> understanding -> connection -> hope -> action
```

The page alternates between large atmospheric moments and practical clarity:

- expansive hero
- concise trust text
- structured offer pathways
- personal portrait moment
- concrete workshop/course feature
- low-risk article/meditation sample
- testimonial reassurance
- quiet relationship invitation
- clear contact ending

## Whitespace

Whitespace is not empty decoration. It creates permission.

Use generous vertical space between sections. Keep reading blocks narrow. Avoid dense clusters of cards. Let images and text sit with air around them. The visitor should never feel visually crowded.

## Typography Rhythm

Typography should feel editorial and calm:

- display serif for emotional statements and page titles
- clean sans for navigation, body copy, forms, and utility labels
- generous line height
- normal letter spacing
- no oversized text inside compact UI

The strongest typographic moments should appear in:

- homepage hero
- section statements
- About introduction
- article titles
- workshop detail title

## Photography Rhythm

Photography should move from direct trust to atmospheric detail:

1. Meet Joanna.
2. See her in a calm setting.
3. Notice hands, notebook, tea, flowers, or room details.
4. See workshop/course context.
5. Return to Joanna at contact.

Current supplied portraits are useful for concept direction but have watermarks. Production should use clean originals matching the same mood.

## Transitions

Transitions should be felt more than noticed:

- sections fade in gently
- images reveal with a soft upward drift
- cards respond with a 2px lift or border change
- accordions open slowly enough to feel composed
- page transitions fade rather than slide aggressively

## Colour Usage

Warm porcelain is the default background. Natural paper is used for forms and repeated item surfaces. Deep charcoal carries text. Sage and mist create quiet tonal shifts. Muted clay is reserved for primary action and warmth.

Avoid large fields of clay or sage. They are accents, not themes.

## Hierarchy

The hierarchy should be obvious without being forceful:

- one dominant idea per viewport
- one primary CTA per major section
- secondary CTAs are text links or quiet outlines
- images support emotional context, not filler
- metadata is visible but subdued

## Storyboard Feeling

The homepage should feel like a slow short film:

```text
Light enters.
A person appears.
A sentence creates recognition.
The path becomes clear.
The visitor sees options.
Trust deepens.
One small action becomes possible.
```
