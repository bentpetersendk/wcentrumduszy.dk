# Project Specification

## Product Vision

W Centrum Duszy is a premium digital platform for Joanna Radek-Petersen. It should feel calm, safe, elegant, and deeply human: closer to a refined wellness studio, Scandinavian interior brand, or luxury spa than to a mystical self-help site.

The website must help visitors understand Joanna's work, trust her presence, and take a gentle next step: read, book, join a workshop, explore a course, or make contact.

## Business Goals

- Establish Joanna as a trusted guide for personal growth, inner calm, and reflective practice.
- Present offers clearly without making the experience feel sales-heavy.
- Support workshops, courses, meditations, articles, testimonials, and future bookings.
- Let Joanna update content herself without code, Git, or GitHub.
- Build a multilingual foundation for Polish first, with Danish and English expansion.
- Create a platform that can grow into paid products, retreats, client resources, and a content library.

## Target Audience

Primary audience: adults seeking calm, clarity, emotional grounding, personal growth, or supportive self-reflection. They may feel busy, overwhelmed, curious, or in transition, and they need the site to feel safe before they engage.

Secondary audience: returning clients, workshop participants, newsletter subscribers, podcast listeners, and people referred by friends.

Tertiary audience: collaborators, retreat venues, therapists, wellness professionals, media, and organizations looking for workshops.

## Positioning

W Centrum Duszy should communicate mature calm rather than spectacle. The brand promise is not instant transformation; it is a steady, elegant invitation into presence and self-connection.

## Website Objectives

- Explain who Joanna is and what W Centrum Duszy offers.
- Help visitors choose between individual sessions, workshops, courses, meditations, and articles.
- Make contact and booking paths clear.
- Build trust through Joanna-owned photography, testimonials, transparent descriptions, and soft but confident writing.
- Support SEO for branded searches, workshops, meditations, personal growth topics, and local availability.
- Provide a CMS that feels as simple as posting to a social page.

## User Journeys

First-time visitor: lands on homepage, feels calm and oriented, learns what Joanna offers, reads About or a relevant offer, then contacts or joins the newsletter.

Workshop seeker: lands on a workshop page from search or social, scans date, location, theme, price, and available places, reads the experience description, then requests or books a place.

Returning client: opens the site for new meditations, articles, course materials, or upcoming events, then signs up or logs in in future phases.

Skeptical visitor: checks About, testimonials, FAQ, and contact details before engaging. The design must answer safety questions through tone, structure, and clarity.

Content reader: arrives through an article, finds thoughtful content, sees related meditations or workshops, then subscribes.

## Content Strategy

Content should be warm, grounded, and specific. Avoid exaggerated claims, spiritual jargon, or pressure. Prefer short paragraphs, plain language, sensory calm, and clear outcomes.

Core content types: homepage sections, About story, offers, workshops, courses, meditations, articles, gallery, testimonials, FAQ, contact, SEO metadata, navigation labels, and footer links.

Every content item should support multilingual fields, draft/published state, scheduling, SEO, and image selection from the asset library.

## Success Metrics

- Contact and booking inquiries.
- Workshop signups.
- Newsletter subscriptions.
- Article engagement and return visits.
- Organic search impressions for brand and offer terms.
- Admin publishing confidence: Joanna can update core pages without help.

## Phase 1 Deliverables

- Complete documentation and architecture.
- Branding asset catalogue.
- Content model and database schema.
- CMS and admin UX specification.
- Technical architecture and future roadmap.

## Roadmap

Phase 1: discovery, specification, asset inventory, information architecture.

Phase 2: visual design, CMS prototype, content migration plan, technical setup.

Phase 3: production website build, SEO setup, accessibility testing, analytics.

Phase 4: booking, payments, courses, meditation library, newsletter, client login.
