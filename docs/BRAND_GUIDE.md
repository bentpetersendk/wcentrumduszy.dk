# Brand Guide

## Brand Essence

W Centrum Duszy should feel safe, calm, trustworthy, present, elegant, and quietly personal. The visual identity must stay grounded. It should never feel theatrical, mystical, occult, or exaggerated.

Design references: Apple clarity, Aesop restraint, Headspace warmth, Muuto softness, Scandinavian interiors, luxury spa rituals, natural light, linen, stone, wood, water, breath.

## Logo

The client-supplied logo should become the basis for the visual identity. Until source files are supplied, the production design should reserve space for a refined wordmark or symbol/wordmark system.

Logo rules:

- Use the primary logo in the header, footer, favicon, and social previews.
- Preserve clear space equal to the height of the logo mark or first capital letter.
- Do not add shadows, gradients, outlines, or decorative effects.
- Prepare light, dark, monochrome, and small-size variants.
- Never combine the logo with generic spiritual symbols unless they are part of the original supplied identity.

## Colour Palette

The palette should be natural, spacious, and premium, with enough contrast for accessibility.

| Role | Colour | Hex | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Warm white | Soft Porcelain | `#F7F4EF` | Page background, calm foundation |
| Paper | Natural Paper | `#FEFCF8` | Content surfaces |
| Ink | Deep Charcoal | `#252525` | Primary text |
| Soft text | Stone Grey | `#6F6A63` | Secondary text |
| Sage | Quiet Sage | `#A8B6A1` | Subtle brand accent |
| Clay | Muted Clay | `#B58B73` | Warm CTA accent |
| Mist | Blue Grey Mist | `#D8E0DF` | Section contrast |
| Line | Warm Line | `#E4DDD3` | Borders and dividers |

Decision: the palette avoids one-note beige or mystical purple. Sage, clay, mist, charcoal, and warm paper create a grounded wellness tone with Scandinavian restraint.

## Typography

Recommended direction:

- Display: a refined serif such as `Cormorant Garamond`, `Canela`, or `Editorial New` if licensed.
- Interface/body: a calm humanist sans such as `Inter`, `Suisse Int'l`, `Avenir`, or `Satoshi`.
- Polish diacritic support is mandatory.
- Danish and English support is mandatory for future expansion.

Usage:

- Large display type only for major page titles and emotionally important statements.
- Body text should be generous, readable, and never tiny.
- Avoid script fonts and decorative spiritual typography.

## Spacing

Use generous whitespace to create breath and trust.

- Base spacing unit: 8px.
- Section padding: 64-120px desktop, 40-72px mobile.
- Content max width: 1120-1200px.
- Reading max width: 680-760px.
- Cards: restrained, 8px radius maximum.

## Photography Style

Use Joanna's own photographs wherever possible.

Preferred photography:

- Natural light.
- Calm interiors.
- Real portraits with eye contact or quiet presence.
- Hands, textures, workshop moments, nature details.
- Honest documentary warmth rather than stock perfection.

Avoid:

- Overly mystical imagery.
- Generic yoga silhouettes.
- Dark dramatic forests.
- Heavy blur, sparkles, crystals, galaxies, or exaggerated symbolism.

## Illustration and Icons

Illustration should be minimal and rare. Use clean line icons for utility only. Icons should clarify actions, not decorate the brand.

## Animation

Animation principles:

- Slow, soft, and purposeful.
- Use fades, gentle reveals, and subtle parallax only when it supports orientation.
- No bouncing, spinning, glittering, or distracting motion.
- Respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.

## Accessibility

- WCAG 2.2 AA target.
- Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for body text.
- Keyboard accessible navigation and forms.
- Visible focus states.
- Alt text for all meaningful images.
- Plain-language error messages.
- No important content hidden only behind motion, hover, or images.

## Mood Boards

Mood board 1: soft interior calm. Materials: linen, pale wood, stone, ceramic, diffused daylight.

Mood board 2: grounded nature. Materials: shoreline, leaves, water, open sky, neutral earth tones.

Mood board 3: premium editorial. Materials: large margins, quiet typography, refined portraits, spare layouts.
