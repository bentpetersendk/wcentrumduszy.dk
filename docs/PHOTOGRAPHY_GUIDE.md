# Photography Guide

## Purpose

This guide extends `BRAND_GUIDE.md` and `assets/branding/ASSET_INVENTORY.md`. Joanna's own images should be the primary visual language. Stock photography is a last resort.

## Current Asset Reality

The repository includes extracted images in:

- `assets/photos/portraits/`
- `assets/photos/website-document/`
- `assets/logo/source/`

The portrait images currently show visible watermark overlays, so they require approval, source confirmation, or replacement with clean originals before production use.

## Homepage Photography

The homepage should use Joanna's presence early. The visitor must meet a real person, not an abstract wellness concept.

Recommended use:

- Hero: calm portrait with natural light and direct or gently off-camera expression.
- Trust section: detail image from a session, hands, tea, notebook, flowers, or interior.
- About preview: warm portrait with enough environmental context.
- Workshop section: Joanna preparing or guiding, not staged performance.
- Newsletter/contact: softer image, possibly seated or writing.

## Portraits

Portraits should feel approachable, elegant, and real.

Facial expressions:

- soft smile
- thoughtful calm
- attentive eye contact
- gentle seriousness when appropriate

Avoid:

- exaggerated bliss
- closed-eye spiritual posing as the main identity
- overly polished corporate headshots
- heavy retouching

## Workshop Photography

Workshop photography should communicate safety and atmosphere.

Capture:

- small groups in calm spaces
- materials on tables
- hands writing
- Joanna listening or guiding
- light, space, and human scale

Avoid showing vulnerable participant faces without explicit consent.

## Nature Photography

Nature can support the brand when it feels grounded and local.

Use:

- water
- soft leaves
- open sky
- paths
- quiet gardens
- natural textures

Avoid:

- dramatic fantasy forests
- galaxies
- moon rituals as visual shorthand
- heavy fog
- mystical symbolism

## Close-Ups

Close-ups should add sensory calm:

- ceramic cup
- notebook
- linen texture
- flowers
- hands
- candle flame only if subtle and not spiritualized
- wood, stone, water

## Lighting

Use natural, diffused light. The site should feel like morning or late afternoon calm, not studio drama.

Avoid harsh contrast, blue shadows, orange filters, and heavy colour grading.

## Clothing

Clothing should feel natural, comfortable, and refined:

- white
- cream
- muted sage
- soft blue
- muted clay
- natural fabrics
- simple silhouettes

Avoid loud patterns where they compete with text or the logo.

## Camera Angles

Use human eye-level angles. Slightly wider environmental portraits help visitors understand Joanna's world. Avoid low-angle authority shots or overly intimate close-ups.

## Composition

Leave negative space for text overlays when images are used in heroes. Keep faces away from crop edges. Compose with calm verticals, soft diagonals, and natural asymmetry.

## Cropping

Desktop:

- Hero images can be wide or split with text if the image remains emotionally central.
- Use 16:9, 4:3, and portrait crops deliberately.

Mobile:

- Protect faces and hands.
- Avoid text over detailed parts of images.
- Prepare separate mobile crops where needed.

## Backgrounds

Preferred backgrounds are bright interiors, plants, curtains, soft furniture, garden paths, and natural textures. Backgrounds should never feel cluttered.

## Stock Photography Rules

Use stock only when there is no Joanna-owned image for the need.

Stock must be:

- quiet
- human-scale
- naturally lit
- non-performative
- free of spiritual cliches
- licensed and documented in `assets/branding/ASSET_INVENTORY.md`
