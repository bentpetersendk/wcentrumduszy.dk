# Design Inspiration

## Purpose

This document extends `BRAND_GUIDE.md` and `DESIGN_SYSTEM.md`. It defines the sensory world of W Centrum Duszy so another agency could design within the brand without meeting Joanna.

## Materials

Linen: signals softness, breath, and natural imperfection.

Ceramic: signals hand-made care and quiet ritual.

Pale wood: signals Scandinavian warmth without heaviness.

Stone: signals steadiness and grounding.

Paper: signals reflection, writing, and personal process.

## Textures

Textures should be nearly invisible. Use them as atmosphere, not decoration. A subtle paper grain or linen-like surface can make the interface feel human, but it must not reduce readability.

## Interior Design

The interior reference is calm, lived-in, and refined: soft chairs, pale curtains, plants, simple tables, a cup, a notebook, natural light.

Why it fits: Joanna's work depends on emotional safety. Interior cues help visitors imagine being held in a real, comfortable space.

## Furniture

Furniture references should be low, soft, tactile, and human-scale. Avoid imposing luxury. The feeling is "you can sit here and breathe," not "look how expensive this is."

## Architecture

Architectural inspiration should use light, thresholds, quiet corridors, windows, and open rooms. These elements support the metaphor of moving inward without using mystical imagery.

## Nature

Nature should be close and observable: leaves, paths, water, sky, gardens, seasonal change. Avoid nature as spectacle.

Why it fits: grounded nature supports the brand's promise of slow attention and real life.

## Editorial Layouts

Use magazine-like restraint:

- generous margins
- strong typographic hierarchy
- carefully placed images
- quiet captions
- long-form readability

Why it fits: editorial design makes Joanna's work feel thoughtful and credible.

## Luxury Spa Inspiration

The spa influence should appear through calm sequencing, sensory detail, and care in transitions. It should never become generic spa stock imagery.

Why it fits: visitors are seeking nervous-system relief, safety, and restoration.

## Japanese Minimalism

Borrow restraint, asymmetry, empty space, and respect for materials. Do not imitate cultural motifs superficially.

Why it fits: the brand needs quiet discipline, not decorative abundance.

## Scandinavian Simplicity

Use functional beauty, natural light, soft neutrals, and honest materials.

Why it fits: Scandinavian simplicity supports trust, clarity, and ease for a non-technical CMS and a broad visitor audience.

## Visual Identity Extensions

Icons: use thin, rounded line icons only for navigation and utility. If custom icons are created, they should be derived from the rhythm of the logo mark without becoming ornamental.

Illustration: avoid character illustrations. If needed, use abstract line drawings of paths, circles, leaves, or breath-like arcs, always minimal.

Image treatments: natural colour, soft contrast, no heavy filters. Use warm paper backgrounds and subtle borders.

Section transitions: use spacing, image rhythm, and quiet background shifts rather than decorative dividers.

Micro animations: slow reveals, soft hover lifts, gentle opacity changes.

Hover behaviour: calm and legible. Cards may lift 2px or reveal a subtle border. Never bounce.

Scrolling behaviour: smooth by default only if it does not interfere with accessibility. No scroll-jacking.

Loading experience: skeletons in warm neutral tones. Avoid dramatic spinners.

Dark mode: not recommended for the initial brand. The emotional world is light, natural, and spacious. A dark mode could be considered later for meditation listening only.
